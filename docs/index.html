Millions Gather in Iran for Funeral of Ayatollah Ali Khamenei

What happened

Huge crowds gathered in Tehran for the start of a days-long funeral for Iran's former Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Khamenei, who ruled Iran for more than three decades, was killed in February by an airstrike at the start of a war involving the US and Israel. The government has shut down the country for two days to encourage public participation in the ceremonies.

Why it matters

Khamenei's death is a major turning point for the region, and the massive public mourning is being used by the Iranian government to project a message of political continuity. Western analysts are watching how the transition of power unfolds during this period of heightened military tension.

What to watch next

Khamenei's body will travel across Iran and Iraq before being buried in the city of Mashhad. Watch for announcements on the selection of a new Supreme Leader by Iran's Assembly of Experts in the coming weeks.

Extreme Heat Disrupts US National Celebrations and Breaks Records Globally

What happened

A dangerous heatwave has blanketed the United States, forcing organizers to cancel high-profile Fourth of July events, including Washington DC's Independence Day parade. More than 165 million people are under heat warnings as temperatures surpass 38 degrees Celsius. Globally, cities like Sydney, Australia, recorded their hottest June on record, while the United Kingdom is bracing for a week-long heatwave.

Why it matters

The extreme weather is straining power grids, threatening public health, and disrupting major public events during the US's 250th anniversary. Scientists warn that these record-breaking temperatures are a direct consequence of global warming driven by greenhouse gas emissions.

What to watch next

Forecasters expect the US heatwave to persist for several days, while the UK's heat health alerts will remain in place through Saturday. Keep an eye on regional power grids for potential blackouts.

India Designates 23 Pakistan-Based Operatives as Terrorists

What happened

The Indian government has officially designated 23 Pakistan-based individuals as terrorists under the country's anti-terror law, the Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act. The home ministry states that these individuals belong to groups like Jaish-e-Mohammed and Lashkar-e-Taiba. Officials accuse them of planning and executing attacks in Jammu and Kashmir.

Why it matters

The designations allow Indian authorities to freeze the assets of these individuals and prosecute anyone who assists them. This move signals India's continued hardline stance against cross-border militancy and keeps diplomatic pressure on Pakistan to curb extremist groups on its soil.

What to watch next

Watch for official responses from Pakistan's foreign ministry. Indian security agencies are expected to share these updated lists with international partners to restrict the suspects' global travel and financial access.

Ukraine Hits Major Russian Oil Terminals Near St. Petersburg

What happened

Ukrainian long-range drones struck a major oil terminal and military facilities near the Russian city of St. Petersburg. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky said the attack targeted infrastructure that funds Russia's war effort. The strikes disrupted local internet services and flights, while Russia launched retaliatory strikes on a gas facility in central Ukraine.

Why it matters

St. Petersburg is hundreds of kilometers away from the Ukrainian border, showing Ukraine's growing capacity to strike deep inside Russian territory. By targeting oil facilities, Ukraine hopes to damage Russia's economy and disrupt fuel supplies used by the Russian military.

What to watch next

NATO leaders will meet at an upcoming summit where European members and Canada are expected to pledge long-term funding for Ukraine's defense, especially as US military commitments remain uncertain.

Mass Protests and Clashes Erupt at Far-Right Party Conference in Germany

What happened

Around 20,000 protesters gathered in the German city of Erfurt to demonstrate against the far-right Alternative for Germany (AfD) party's national conference. Riot police clashed with protesters who tried to block roads and prevent party delegates from entering. Inside the venue, the AfD met to elect its leadership.

Why it matters

The AfD has seen a rise in popularity, causing concern among mainstream political parties, trade unions, and civil rights groups. The protests highlight deep political divisions in Germany over issues like immigration and national identity.

What to watch next

The AfD will complete its leadership elections this weekend. Analysts will watch how the party performs in regional elections in eastern Germany scheduled for September, where the AfD is polling strongly.

Tuareg Rebels Launch Coordinated Attacks in Northern Mali

What happened

Tuareg-led rebels and Islamist militants launched coordinated attacks on several towns and military bases in northern Mali. The rebels targeted areas where Malian government troops and Russian fighters are stationed, as well as a local prison. Mali's army later claimed that its forces successfully repelled the assaults and regained control.

Why it matters

Mali has faced instability since a military coup in 2021. The military government has increasingly relied on Russian private security forces to fight insurgencies after asking French and UN troops to leave. These fresh attacks show that the rebels still hold significant capability to challenge the state's authority.

What to watch next

Watch for updates on casualties and the security situation from Mali's military leadership. Regional security analysts are monitoring whether these clashes will displace more civilians toward neighboring countries.
