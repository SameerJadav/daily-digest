Iran begins multi-day funeral for late Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei

What happened

Iran has begun a six-day public funeral for its late Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, who was killed in a joint US-Israeli air strike in February. Millions of mourners gathered in the capital city of Tehran to view the caskets of Khamenei and his family members. The funeral will travel to several holy cities in Iran and Iraq before the final burial. Meanwhile, his successor and son, Mojtaba Khamenei, has not been seen in public for months.

Why it matters

Khamenei ruled Iran for 37 years. His death during the US-Israel war has heightened regional tensions. The massive, highly symbolic funeral is intended to project national unity, religious devotion, and resistance to Western powers.

What to watch next

Watch whether Mojtaba Khamenei makes his first public appearance before the funeral ceremonies end with a burial in the city of Mashhad on Thursday, July 9.

Words to know

Ayatollah EYE-uh-TOH-luh
A high-ranking title given to Shia Muslim religious leaders. In this story, it refers to Ali Khamenei, who held the ultimate political and religious authority in Iran.
Shia SHEE-uh
One of the two main branches of Islam. Iran is a majority-Shia nation, and its supreme leader is a major spiritual figure for Shia Muslims worldwide.
IRGC eye-ar-jee-see
The Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, an elite branch of Iran's military. They are responsible for defending the country's Islamic system and have expanded their power significantly over the decades.

India designates 23 Pakistan-based operatives as terrorists under anti-terror law

What happened

The Indian Ministry of Home Affairs has added 23 Pakistan-based individuals to its official list of designated terrorists under its anti-terror law. These individuals are allegedly associated with militant groups like Lashkar-e-Taiba and Jaish-e-Mohammad. India accuses them of smuggling weapons via drones, recruiting youth through social media, and planning major attacks in Jammu and Kashmir.

Why it matters

The newly designated individuals include senior commanders, recruiters, and relatives of prominent militant leaders. This update brings the total number of individuals designated as terrorists by India to 80, which allows the government to freeze their assets and curb their operations.

What to watch next

Watch for any official response from Pakistan regarding these designations and whether India increases security patrols along the Line of Control to intercept drone-delivered weapons.

Words to know

Lashkar-e-Taiba LUSH-kar-ay-TIE-yuh-buh
A Pakistan-based militant group. India and several other countries blame the group for numerous attacks, including the 2008 Mumbai attacks.
Jaish-e-Mohammad JAYSH-ay-mo-HAM-mad
A Pakistan-based jihadist group. The group seeks to separate Kashmir from India and has been blamed for major attacks on Indian military bases.

Ukraine strikes key Russian oil terminals near St. Petersburg

What happened

Ukraine launched a wave of long-range drones targeting Russian energy facilities near St. Petersburg, about 900 kilometers from the Ukrainian border. The drones struck a major oil terminal in St. Petersburg and another port facility in Vysotsk, which handle vital fuel exports. Ukraine claims its drone campaign has now disabled over 42% of Russia's oil refining capacity, forcing Moscow to restrict petrol exports to prevent domestic shortages. Meanwhile, Russia claimed it captured the strategic eastern Ukrainian town of Kostiantynivka, but Ukraine's military strongly denied the claim.

Why it matters

This is one of Ukraine's deepest airstrikes since Russia's 2022 invasion. Ukraine is targeting Russian energy infrastructure to disrupt the fuel revenues that fund Moscow's war effort.

What to watch next

Watch for any Russian retaliatory strikes against Ukrainian gas production facilities and whether Russia extends its ban on petrol exports beyond current levels.

Words to know

refining capacity ree-FINE-ing kuh-PASS-ih-tee
The maximum amount of crude oil a facility can process into usable products like petrol or diesel. Ukraine is targeting these facilities to choke Russia's fuel supplies and economy.
Baltic Fleet BAWL-tik fleet
A major branch of the Russian Navy based in the Baltic Sea. Ukraine claims to have hit its Kronstadt naval base near St. Petersburg during the drone attacks.

Severe heatwaves shatter records and disrupt events across the US, Europe, and Australia

What happened

Extreme heatwaves are breaking historical temperature records across the United States, Europe, and Australia. In the US, blistering temperatures cancelled major Fourth of July events, while Sydney, Australia, recorded its hottest June since record-keeping began in 1859. Scientists say these severe heatwaves are direct consequences of global warming caused by human activities, such as burning fossil fuels. The unseasonal winter warmth in Australia is also drying out soils, which experts warn could amplify bushfire risks in the upcoming spring.

Why it matters

The widespread and intense nature of these heatwaves represents a significant threat to public health, infrastructure, and agriculture, signaling that extreme weather is becoming the new normal.

What to watch next

Watch for updated global temperature reports for July 2026 to see if this month breaks the all-time global heat record, and monitor whether US cities experience major power grid failures during the ongoing heatwave.

Words to know

heat dome heet dohm
A weather phenomenon where a high-pressure system traps hot ocean air like a lid, leading to prolonged and extreme heatwaves.
mean temperature meen TEM-pruh-cher
The average of all temperature readings taken over a specific period, such as a day or a month. Scientists use it to track long-term climate trends.

Pope Leo urges protection for migrants and praises US history of immigration

What happened

Pope Leo XIV visited the Italian island of Lampedusa, a major entry point for Mediterranean migrants, where he called on European leaders to create long-term plans to protect and integrate immigrants. He also issued a video message and a letter marking the 250th anniversary of the United States, praising its history of welcoming immigrants. The Pope’s remarks represent an implicit clash with US President Donald Trump, who has championed mass deportations and visa bans. Pope Leo, who was born in the US, asserted that defending human life includes welcoming and assisting immigrants with dignity.

Why it matters

This represents an escalating ideological dispute between the Vatican and the US administration over immigration and foreign policy. Trump has criticized the Pope as weak on crime, while the Pope has actively condemned Trump's immigration policies and the US-led war in Iran.

What to watch next

Watch for any formal response from the White House regarding the Pope's comments, and whether the Pope announces a visit to the US later this year.

Words to know

Lampedusa lam-puh-DOO-suh
A small Italian island in the Mediterranean Sea. It is closer to North Africa than mainland Italy, making it a primary destination for migrants attempting the dangerous sea crossing.
Liberty Medal LIB-er-tee MED-uhl
An annual American award given to individuals who demonstrate leadership in the pursuit of freedom. Pope Leo was honored with this medal during his address to the National Constitution Center.

Tuareg rebels launch coordinated attacks on towns and military bases in Mali

What happened

A coalition of Tuareg separatists and Islamist rebels launched coordinated attacks on five locations across Mali, targeting military bases and a major prison near the capital, Bamako. Mali’s military said it killed dozens of attackers and has the situation under control, but rebels claimed fighting is ongoing. Mali has been gripped by a decade-long insurgency. The military junta that took power in coups in 2020 and 2021 promised to restore stability, turning away from French military help toward Russian support. However, these attacks, following a major offensive in April that killed the country's defense minister, indicate that the rebels are growing more coordinated and bolder, stretching the military's resources.

Why it matters

The growing cooperation between Tuareg separatists and Islamist groups, combined with high-profile attacks near the capital, raises serious concerns about the Malian junta's ability to maintain control of the country.

What to watch next

Watch for the military junta's official response to the prison attack near Bamako, and whether Russian paramilitary reinforcements are deployed to help the Malian army secure the northern towns of Anefis and Aguelhoc.

Words to know

Tuareg TWAH-reg
A nomadic ethnic group living across the Saharan region of North Africa. A separatist faction, the Azawad Liberation Front, has been fighting for years to establish an independent state in northern Mali.
JNIM jay-nim
An al-Qaeda-affiliated militant group operating in the Sahel region. They seek to impose strict Islamic rule and have recently coordinated with Tuareg rebels despite having different ideologies.
