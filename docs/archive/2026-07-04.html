Massive Public Funeral Ceremonies Begin for Slain Iranian Leader Ali Khamenei

What happened

Iran has started a six-day public funeral for its late Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Millions of mourners gathered in Tehran to view his coffin alongside family members killed with him. Khamenei was assassinated in February 2026 during a joint US-Israeli air strike, which started the ongoing war.

Why it matters

Khamenei ruled Iran for 37 years as its absolute spiritual and political authority. The massive funeral is designed to show the regime's stability and popular support to the world. However, many Iranians remain deeply divided, remembering Khamenei for his security forces' deadly crackdowns on public protests.

What to watch next

The funeral procession will move to the Iranian city of Qom and then to Shia holy sites in Iraq on Monday, July 6, before a final burial in Mashhad on Thursday, July 9, 2026. Watch to see if his son and successor, Mojtaba Khamenei, makes his first public appearance.

Words to know

Ayatollah eye-uh-TOH-luh
A high-ranking Shia Muslim cleric. In this story, it refers to Ali Khamenei, who was Iran's supreme religious and political leader.
Shia SHEE-uh
One of the two main branches of Islam. Iran is a majority-Shia nation, and Khamenei was a key spiritual figure for this global community.
IRGC eye-aar-jee-SEE
The Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, an elite branch of Iran's military. They are responsible for internal security and regional operations, and oversaw the crackdown on protesters in Iran.

Extreme Heat Disrupts US 250th Independence Day Celebrations

What happened

Record-breaking heatwaves across the United States have forced the cancellation or delay of major July 4th events, including the parade in Washington DC. Temperatures hit 103 degrees Fahrenheit, with humidity making it feel much hotter. Dozens of people were treated for heat-related illnesses at a national festival hosted by President Donald Trump.

Why it matters

The heat disrupted historic celebrations marking 250 years since the US declared independence from Great Britain. Despite the heat, President Trump gave a highly political holiday speech at Mount Rushmore. He broke with traditional unifying messages to warn that progressives winning local elections are bringing a communist menace to America.

What to watch next

Watch for President Trump's planned outdoor speech and record-breaking fireworks show in Washington DC on Saturday evening, July 4, 2026, to see if heat conditions force further changes.

Words to know

Heat index HEET IN-deks
A measure of how hot it feels when relative humidity is combined with the actual air temperature. High heat indices of up to 115 degrees Fahrenheit made outdoor celebrations dangerous.
Semiquincentennial sem-ee-kwin-sen-TEN-ee-uhl
A 250th anniversary. This year marks 250 years since the US declared independence from Great Britain in 1776.

Ukraine Strikes Russian Oil Terminal and Port in St. Petersburg

What happened

Ukrainian drones struck a major oil terminal and a Baltic Sea port near St. Petersburg, Russia's second-largest city. The target is located about 900 kilometers from Ukrainian-held territory. Meanwhile, Russia claimed to have captured the strategic eastern Ukrainian town of Kostiantynivka, but Ukraine's military denied this, saying fighting is still underway.

Why it matters

Ukraine is targeting energy infrastructure to choke off the oil revenues Russia uses to fund its invasion. Ukrainian officials claim they have disabled nearly 43 percent of Russia's oil refining capacity, leading to domestic fuel shortages in Russia. In response, Russia has restricted its fuel exports and retaliated by bombing Ukrainian gas facilities.

What to watch next

Watch for whether independent military analysts can verify who controls the contested town of Kostiantynivka in Donetsk over the coming week.

Words to know

Refining capacity ree-FY-ning kuh-PASS-ih-tee
The maximum amount of crude oil a facility can process into usable products like petrol or diesel. Ukraine claims to have disabled more than 40 percent of Russia's capacity.
Baltic Fleet BAWL-tik FLEET
A major division of the Russian Navy stationed in the Baltic Sea. Ukraine claims to have hit its key base in Kronstadt during the drone wave.

India Designates 23 Pakistan-Based Operatives as Terrorists

What happened

India's Union Ministry of Home Affairs has added 23 Pakistan-based individuals to its official list of designated terrorists. The government claims these men are associated with militant groups like Lashkar-e-Taiba and Jaish-e-Mohammad. The list now contains a total of 80 individuals designated as terrorists by India.

Why it matters

The Indian government accuses these individuals of running training camps, coordinating cross-border infiltration, and smuggling weapons into Jammu and Kashmir using drones. Several of the newly designated men are linked to major past attacks, including a 2016 assault on an Indian Army camp in Nagrota. India uses these official designations to help track and disrupt international financing networks for these groups.

What to watch next

Watch for any official diplomatic response from Pakistan's government, which has historically denied supporting these militant groups.

Words to know

Lashkar-e-Taiba LUSH-kur-ay-TIE-buh
A Pakistan-based militant group responsible for several major attacks in India, including the 2008 Mumbai attacks.
Line of Control LYN uv kun-TROLE
The highly militarized de facto border that divides the region of Kashmir between India and Pakistan.

Pope Leo XIV Urges Protection for Migrants on Visit to Italian Island

What happened

Pope Leo XIV visited the Italian island of Lampedusa, a major landing spot for migrants crossing the Mediterranean Sea from Africa. He urged European leaders to help integrate arrivals rather than simply blocking them. The Pope also sent a holiday message to the US praising its history of welcoming immigrants.

Why it matters

The Pope’s comments put him at odds with political leaders in Europe and the US, who are toughening border restrictions. The European Union recently approved rules giving countries broader powers to detain migrants, while US President Donald Trump has pushed for mass deportations. This tension has escalated since the Pope criticized the US-backed war in Iran.

What to watch next

Watch to see if Pope Leo schedules an official diplomatic visit to the United States in the second half of 2026, which he has so far avoided.

Words to know

Pontiff PON-tiff
Another title for the Pope, who is the global head of the Roman Catholic Church.
Lampedusa lahm-peh-DOO-sah
A small Italian island in the Mediterranean Sea that sits close to North Africa, making it a frequent destination for migrants.

Rebel Alliance Launches Coordinated Attacks Across Mali

What happened

A coalition of Tuareg separatists and Islamist militants launched coordinated attacks on several towns and a prison in Mali. The Malian military claimed to have repelled the assaults and killed more than 20 rebels. However, the rebel groups claimed they successfully captured several army positions.

Why it matters

Mali's military junta took power in coups in 2020 and 2021 by promising to end a long-running regional insurgency. Since then, the junta has expelled French military forces and brought in Russian mercenaries. These latest attacks show that the rebel alliance is becoming more organized and is successfully challenging government control.

What to watch next

Watch for updates from the northern towns of Anefis and Aguelhoc to see if government and Russian forces can maintain their hold on these key outposts.

Words to know

Tuareg TWAH-reg
A historically nomadic ethnic group spread across the Sahara Desert, some of whom have fought for an independent state in northern Mali.
Junta HOON-tuh
A military group that rules a country by force after taking power in a coup. Mali has been ruled by a junta since 2020.
