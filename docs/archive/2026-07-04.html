Iran Begins Multi-Day Funeral for Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei

What happened

Iran has commenced extensive funeral ceremonies for its late Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Thousands of mourners gathered in Tehran's Grand Mosalla, with plans for the body to travel across Iran and Iraq for further ceremonies until Monday. Some outlets, including NPR and DW, reported that Khamenei was killed in an airstrike in February at the start of a conflict with the US and Israel, but other sources like the BBC and Al Jazeera did not specify a cause of death or mention an airstrike.

Why it matters

Khamenei ruled Iran for over three decades and was a pivotal figure in the country's political and religious landscape. His death creates uncertainty around succession and could influence Iran's domestic and foreign policies, especially concerning regional tensions. The massive turnout, whether spontaneous or state-organized, aims to demonstrate national unity and reverence for the leader.

What to watch next

The funeral processions will continue for several days, ending with burial in Mashhad. Discussions about his successor are expected to intensify, which could indicate the future direction of Iran's leadership.

Extreme Heat Disrupts US and Europe, Breaks Records

What happened

A dangerous heatwave is affecting over 165 million people across the US, leading to the cancellation of Fourth of July events in cities like Washington D.C. and Philadelphia, with temperatures expected to remain at 38 degrees Celsius. Europe is also experiencing record-breaking temperatures, with June smashing previous highs and a week-long heatwave forecast for England. Sydney, Australia, recorded its hottest June since 1859.

Why it matters

This widespread extreme weather highlights the immediate and tangible impacts of climate change on daily life, public health, and infrastructure across multiple continents. The cancellations and warnings underscore the severe risks to vulnerable populations and the need for adaptation strategies. Scientists warn that these record temperatures are a sign of future trends.

What to watch next

Authorities in affected regions will continue to issue heat health alerts and implement measures to protect citizens. Weather forecasts will indicate how long these extreme conditions will persist and whether more records will be broken.

Ukraine Strikes Russian Oil Terminal Near St. Petersburg

What happened

Ukraine has reportedly launched drone attacks on a major oil terminal and military facilities near St. Petersburg, Russia. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky's office stated that these strikes target key infrastructure that generates revenue for Russia's war efforts. The attacks caused disruptions to internet services and flights in St. Petersburg.

Why it matters

These long-range strikes represent an escalation in Ukraine's strategy to inflict economic damage on Russia and hinder its military operations. By targeting facilities deep within Russian territory, Ukraine aims to disrupt logistical chains and financial resources supporting the war, potentially increasing pressure on Moscow.

What to watch next

Russia's military and political response to these attacks will be closely watched. Further long-range strikes by Ukraine into Russian territory could indicate a sustained campaign to disrupt its war economy.

Pope Leo Urges Greater Protection for Migrants, Praises US History

What happened

Pope Leo visited the Italian island of Lampedusa, a key entry point for migrants into Europe, where he paid tribute to those who have died at sea. The pontiff urged Europe and the United States to increase efforts to protect and integrate migrants, implicitly rebuking current policies and leaders. During a speech, he praised the US history of welcoming immigrants, in remarks interpreted by some outlets as a criticism of President Donald Trump.

Why it matters

The Pope's statements highlight the ongoing humanitarian crisis of migration and serve as a moral call to action for governments. His remarks are seen as politically significant due to their direct engagement with controversial policies and leaders in the US and Europe. This puts pressure on nations to reconsider their approach to migration.

What to watch next

Reactions from European and US political leaders to the Pope's message will be important. Future statements from the Vatican or engagements by Pope Leo on global migration issues could further shape international dialogue.

India Designates 23 Pakistan-Based Individuals as 'Terrorists'

What happened

The Indian government has designated 23 individuals based in Pakistan as "terrorists" under its anti-terror law. These individuals are reportedly associated with groups like Jaish-e-Mohammed and Lashkar-e-Taiba and are linked to various attacks in Jammu and Kashmir. This action allows for legal measures against their assets and activities.

Why it matters

This move underscores India's proactive stance against individuals it believes are involved in cross-border terrorism, reflecting a continued focus on national security. It could also lead to increased diplomatic tensions with Pakistan, as such designations are often a point of contention between the two nations.

What to watch next

Pakistan's official response to these designations will be significant. India may pursue further actions, potentially involving international cooperation, to address the activities of these designated individuals and their associated groups.

Thousands Protest Against Far-Right AfD Party Conference in Germany

What happened

Thousands of protesters gathered in Erfurt, central Germany, to demonstrate against the annual conference of the far-right Alternative for Germany (AfD) party. Riot police were deployed and clashed with some demonstrators who attempted to block roads to prevent AfD delegates from attending the event. Local police reported that 20,000 people were protesting.

Why it matters

These protests highlight the growing political polarization within Germany and significant public opposition to the AfD's agenda and increasing influence. The party's conference, held on a key historical date, further amplifies concerns about its ideology and potential impact on German democracy.

What to watch next

The outcomes of the AfD's conference, including leadership elections, will indicate the party's strategic direction. Future public opinion polls and upcoming regional elections will show whether the protests impact the AfD's support base.

Sudanese City of El Obeid Under Constant Drone Attacks Amid Intense Fighting

What happened

Aid workers describe a dire situation in El Obeid, Sudan, which has become a crucial battleground in the conflict between the Sudanese Armed Forces and the paramilitary Rapid Support Forces (RSF). The city is reportedly under constant drone attacks, with one aid volunteer stating that 40 to 45 drones are a "norm." Recent strikes hit schools and fuel stations, killing over 20 people, including students.

Why it matters

The intense fighting and drone attacks in El Obeid are causing high civilian casualties and extensive damage to essential infrastructure, exacerbating a severe humanitarian crisis. This conflict continues largely outside global attention, leading to immense suffering and displacement for the Sudanese people.

What to watch next

Aid organizations will likely issue further reports on the humanitarian situation in El Obeid and other conflict zones in Sudan. International efforts to broker a ceasefire or facilitate humanitarian access will be crucial to alleviating the crisis.
