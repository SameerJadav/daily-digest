Iran Begins Days-Long Funeral for Slain Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei

What happened

Huge crowds of mourners gathered in Tehran for the start of a six-day funeral procession for Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Khamenei, who ruled Iran for 37 years, was killed in February by an airstrike at the start of a war involving the US and Israel. The government shut down public offices for two days to allow the public to attend the ceremonies, which will end with his burial in the holy city of Mashhad.

Why it matters

Khamenei was the ultimate political and religious authority in Iran, making all final decisions on foreign policy and state matters. His death during an active conflict has raised fears of a power vacuum and further regional escalation. The state is using the massive funeral to project stability, unity, and a message of defiance against its adversaries.

What to watch next

The funeral ceremonies are scheduled to conclude with Khamenei's burial in Mashhad on Wednesday, July 8, after which the regime's assembly must finalize the process of choosing a permanent successor.

Words to know

Ayatollah eye-uh-TOH-luh
A high-ranking Shia Muslim cleric in Iran. In Iran's system, the Supreme Leader is always a senior Ayatollah who holds absolute power over the military and government.
Strait of Hormuz STRAYT of hor-MOOZ
A narrow, strategic waterway between the Persian Gulf and the Gulf of Oman. It is a critical global shipping route through which a third of the world's liquefied natural gas and 20% of global oil consumption passes.
Theocracy thee-OK-ruh-see
A form of government in which religious leaders rule in the name of God. Iran has been an Islamic theocracy since its 1979 revolution.

US Department of Justice Seeks to Dismiss Adani Charges Permanently

What happened

The United States Department of Justice asked a federal judge to permanently drop all criminal charges against Indian billionaire Gautam Adani and his associates. The motion came after federal judge Nicholas Garaufis demanded a clear explanation for why prosecutors wanted to end the case. The government told the court that the bribery and fraud indictment 'should never have been brought' in the first place.

Why it matters

In late 2023, US prosecutors accused Adani of paying hundreds of millions of dollars in bribes to Indian officials to secure lucrative solar energy contracts. The charges caused Adani Group companies to lose billions of dollars in market value and strained US-India business ties. Dropping the charges permanently is a massive victory for Adani and clears a major legal cloud over his global conglomerate.

What to watch next

Judge Nicholas Garaufis is expected to issue a final ruling on whether to officially and permanently dismiss the indictment in the coming days.

Words to know

Indictment in-DYT-munt
A formal written charge or accusation that a person has committed a serious crime. It is issued by a grand jury or court to start a criminal trial.
Department of Justice dih-PART-munt of JUSS-tiss
The executive department of the United States government responsible for enforcing federal laws and administering justice.
Conglomerate kun-GLOM-ur-it
A multi-industry company that owns a controlling stake in a number of smaller, seemingly unrelated companies. Adani Group is a major infrastructure and energy conglomerate.

US Marks 250th Anniversary Amid Historic Heatwave and Cancelled Events

What happened

The United States marked its 250th Independence Day under a dangerous heatwave that forced the cancellation of major celebrations across the East Coast. Over 165 million Americans faced temperatures soaring past 38 degrees Celsius, leading organizers to cancel the flagship parade in Washington, DC. On the political front, President Donald Trump issued 11 pardons, including to nine people who violated the Clean Air Act, and used a speech at Mount Rushmore to criticize illegal immigration.

Why it matters

The extreme heat is a stark reminder of the accelerating effects of global climate change, which scientists say is making heatwaves longer and more intense. Trump's pardons of environmental violators on the same weekend highlighted deep political divisions over environmental protection and climate policy.

What to watch next

The US National Weather Service expects the extreme heat and humidity to persist across the East Coast and Midwest through the middle of next week, keeping heat safety alerts active.

Words to know

Semiquincentennial sem-ee-kwin-sen-TEN-ee-ul
A 250th anniversary. The term is being used to mark the United States' 250 years since declaring independence in 1776.
Clean Air Act kleen AIR akt
A major United States federal law designed to control air pollution on a national level. It regulates emissions from hazardous sources like trucks and factories.
Pardon PAR-dun
A government decision to allow a person who has been convicted of a crime to be free and absolved of that conviction. In the US, the president has broad powers to issue federal pardons.

India Designates 23 Pakistan-Based Operatives as Terrorists

What happened

The Indian government officially designated 23 Pakistan-based individuals as 'terrorists' under the country's strict anti-terror law, the Unlawful Activities Prevention Act. The Union Home Ministry accused the individuals of being associated with banned groups, including Jaish-e-Mohammed and Lashkar-e-Taiba. The ministry claims these operatives have been actively involved in planning and executing attacks in Jammu and Kashmir, as well as smuggling weapons across the border.

Why it matters

This mass designation is one of the largest single listings by the Indian government. It allows law enforcement agencies to freeze any assets these individuals might hold in India and prosecute anyone who provides them financial or material support. It also increases diplomatic pressure on Pakistan to shut down safe havens for these groups.

What to watch next

India's security agencies will monitor international financial channels to track and block assets linked to the newly designated individuals, while the ministry prepares to raise these listings at upcoming international counter-terrorism forums.

Words to know

Unlawful Activities Prevention Act un-LAWF-ul ak-TIV-ih-teez pri-VEN-shun akt
India's primary anti-terror law, often abbreviated as UAPA. It gives the central government broad powers to designate individuals and organizations as terrorists without a trial.
Jaish-e-Mohammed JYSH-eh-moh-HAM-ud
An active Pakistan-based militant group seeking to separate Kashmir from India. It has been banned by India, the UN, and the US.

Mali's Army Battles Coordinated Rebel Attacks in the North

What happened

Tuareg-led separatist rebels and Islamist groups launched coordinated attacks on several towns and military bases in northern Mali. The rebel coalition said they targeted areas where Malian government troops and Russian private military fighters are stationed. Mali's military leadership later announced that they successfully repelled the assaults and regained full control of the affected towns, though they did not release casualty figures.

Why it matters

Mali has been unstable since a 2012 coup, after which Tuareg rebels and Al-Qaeda-linked groups seized parts of the north. The current military junta broke ties with Western allies like France and instead hired Russian mercenaries to help fight the insurgency. The renewed rebel offensive shows that the security situation remains highly fragile despite Russian military assistance.

What to watch next

Independent monitors and humanitarian groups are expected to release verified civilian casualty figures and displacement numbers from the affected northern regions in the coming days.

Words to know

Tuareg TWAH-reg
An ethnic group living primarily in the Sahara desert across North and West Africa. Some Tuareg groups in northern Mali have fought for years to establish an independent homeland called Azawad.
Junta HOON-tah
A military group that rules a country after taking power by force. Mali has been governed by a military junta since a coup in 2020.

Clashes Erupt in Germany Over Far-Right Party Conference

What happened

Riot police clashed with thousands of left-wing protesters in the eastern German city of Erfurt as they tried to block the national conference of the far-right Alternative for Germany party. Over 20,000 demonstrators blocked roads and attempted to prevent delegates from entering the venue. Police deployed water cannons and made multiple arrests to clear the way for the conference, where the party was electing its leadership.

Why it matters

The Alternative for Germany, known as the AfD, has surged in popularity, particularly in eastern Germany, despite being classified as a suspected extremist group by German intelligence services. The massive scale of the protests highlights the deep and tense political polarization in Germany over immigration, national identity, and the rise of far-right politics.

What to watch next

The AfD is expected to finalize its national leadership team and set its policy platform for upcoming state elections in eastern Germany later this year.

Words to know

Alternative for Germany AHL-ter-nah-teev for JUR-muh-nee
A right-wing to far-right political party in Germany, commonly known as the AfD. It opposes immigration, the European Union, and current green energy policies.
Polarization poh-luh-rye-ZAY-shun
The division of a society into two sharply contrasting groups with highly opposing opinions and beliefs. Germany is experiencing intense political polarization over the rise of the AfD.
