US and Iran exchange heavy fire as June ceasefire collapses

What happened

UPDATE: The US military launched a second, larger wave of airstrikes against Iran on Wednesday, following an initial round that hit over 80 targets. In response, Iran's elite military force, the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, launched strikes against US military facilities in Bahrain and Kuwait. This intense exchange follows Iranian attacks on three commercial cargo ships in the Strait of Hormuz, which led US President Donald Trump to declare the June 14 ceasefire over. Previously, we were watching for any Iranian military retaliation and whether the US would wind down hostilities.

Why it matters

This escalation threatens to pull both nations back into a full-scale war. The Strait of Hormuz is a vital international shipping lane, and the clashes have already caused global oil prices to rise sharply. Additionally, the US has revoked a temporary sanctions waiver that had allowed Iran to export oil, removing Iran's primary incentive to maintain the peace.

What to watch next

Watch for the next moves by the US military regarding President Trump's threat to seize Iran's strategic Kharg Island, and check if regional oil prices continue to climb on Thursday, July 9.

Words to know

Sanctions waiver SANGK-shuhnz WAY-ver
A legal permission from a government allowing businesses to bypass certain trade bans. The US revoked this permission, stopping Iran from legally exporting oil.
Precision munitions pri-SIZH-uhn myoo-NISH-uhnz
Highly accurate, guided weapons designed to hit a specific target while minimizing collateral damage. The US military used these to target Iranian air defenses and vessels.

US and Canada charge Lawrence Bishnoi gang over murder of Sikh activist

What happened

US and Canadian federal authorities have charged 37 members of a notorious Indian criminal group in a joint operation called Operation Hard Ball. Among those charged is Lawrence Bishnoi, a prominent gang leader currently held in a high-security prison in Gujarat. The US Department of Justice accuses Bishnoi of using smuggled mobile phones and encrypted apps like WhatsApp from his prison cell to orchestrate the 2023 assassination of Canadian Sikh activist Hardeep Singh Nijjar.

Why it matters

Nijjar's murder in Vancouver severely damaged diplomatic ties between Canada and India, after Canada's former prime minister alleged Indian government involvement. While India denies any foreign interference, Canadian police believe an Indian consular official assisted the gang. These charges highlight how local Indian gang networks are allegedly operating globally from behind bars to extort victims and target political opponents.

What to watch next

Watch for any formal statement from the Indian government regarding these international charges, and look for progress on extraditing key fugitive Satinderjeet Singh, also known as Goldy Brar, from the US.

Words to know

Indictment in-DYT-muhnt
A formal written charge or accusation that a person has committed a serious crime. The US court issued this against Bishnoi and his associates.
Transnational repression trans-NASH-uh-nuhl ri-PRESH-uhn
When a government or its proxies target, harass, or kill political dissidents living in another country. Canadian intelligence has accused India of using these tactics.
Extortion ik-STOR-shuhn
The practice of obtaining money or goods from someone through force, threats, or intimidation. The Bishnoi gang is accused of using this to demand five million dollars from victims in the US.
Khalistan KHAH-li-stahn
A proposed independent homeland for Sikhs in the Punjab region. Nijjar was a prominent leader of this movement, which India classifies as extremist.

Marine Le Pen launches presidential bid despite embezzlement conviction

What happened

French far-right leader Marine Le Pen has launched her 2027 presidential campaign after a Paris appeals court cleared her path to run. The court shortened her ban on seeking public office to 45 months, which she has already partially served. However, the court also upheld her conviction for embezzling European Parliament funds and sentenced her to wear an electronic monitoring tag for a year. Le Pen has appealed to France's highest court, which temporarily suspends the tag sentence during the appeal process.

Why it matters

Le Pen has finished second in France's last two presidential elections, and her party is currently polling very high. Her opponents have criticized her campaign launch, comparing her defiance of the judiciary to Donald Trump's political tactics. If her final appeal fails during the election next year, she could be forced to campaign while wearing an electronic ankle monitor.

What to watch next

Watch for the final ruling of France's highest court, the Court of Cassation, which is expected to decide on Le Pen's appeal before the presidential election begins in April 2027.

Words to know

Embezzlement em-BEZ-uhl-muhnt
The illegal act of stealing or misusing money that has been placed in your trust. Le Pen was convicted of misusing European Union funds to pay her political party's staff.
Court of Cassation kas-SAY-shuhn
The highest court of appeal in France. It reviews whether lower courts applied the law correctly but does not re-examine the facts of the case.
Electronic tag ih-lek-TRON-ik tag
An electronic device worn around the ankle that tracks a person's location to ensure they follow court-ordered restrictions.

Massive Telstra telecom outage paralyzes Australian transport and emergency calls

What happened

A major 12-hour outage at Telstra, Australia's largest telecommunications provider, disrupted critical services across the country. A software glitch caused the network's time-keeping servers, which sync systems to the nanosecond, to reset. This error blocked over 300 emergency calls, halted payment systems for 80,000 businesses, and forced the cancellation of regional train services in Victoria and New South Wales.

Why it matters

The outage highlights how vulnerable modern societies are to single-point software failures in digital infrastructure. Emergency backup systems did not fully connect some callers, forcing Telstra to conduct hundreds of safety welfare checks on people in traumatic situations. The incident has drawn heavy criticism from government officials and sparked a national regulatory investigation.

What to watch next

Watch for the results of the investigation launched by the Australian Communications and Media Authority, and monitor when Victoria's regional train services return to full normal operations.

Words to know

Triple-zero TRIP-uhl ZEER-oh
The primary emergency telephone number in Australia, similar to 112 in India. It failed to connect for hundreds of callers during the network crash.
Redundancy ri-DUN-duhn-see
The inclusion of extra components or backup systems to prevent complete network failure if one part stops working.
GPS node jee-pee-es nohd
A critical server in a telecom network that uses satellite signals to keep time highly synchronized across all devices.

Kerala suspends Wayanad tunnel road project after fatal landslide

What happened

The Kerala state government has halted work on the Wayanad tunnel road project following a fatal landslide at Kalladi near Meppadi. The landslide killed three people, left five missing, and forced at least 129 people into emergency relief camps. Chief Minister V.D. Satheesan visited the site and announced a multi-pronged technical and legal investigation into the disaster, alongside cash relief for victims' families.

Why it matters

Wayanad is highly prone to ecological disasters, and local experts had warned that major construction in fragile hill zones could trigger landslides. The suspension highlights the growing conflict in India between building infrastructure and protecting ecologically sensitive mountain areas.

What to watch next

Watch for the initial findings of the state's multi-pronged cabinet investigation into the disaster, and monitor whether search teams locate the five missing residents in the coming days.

Words to know

Ex gratia eks GRAY-shee-uh
A payment made as a favor or gift rather than because of a legal obligation. The Kerala government promised this cash relief to the victims' families.
Debris slip deh-BREE slip
A type of landslide where soil, rocks, and mud rapidly slide down a slope, often triggered by heavy rain or construction work.
