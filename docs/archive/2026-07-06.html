Super Typhoon Bavi lashes US Pacific islands with catastrophic winds

What happened

Super Typhoon Bavi has made landfall on the US Pacific territories of Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands, bringing winds of up to 290 km/h. Local officials have opened emergency shelters, which are quickly filling up, and suspended port operations and flights. The island of Rota is expected to take a direct hit, with weather officials warning it could be left uninhabitable for weeks.

Why it matters

This is the 11th category four or five storm to hit US territory in the past decade, a major increase from previous decades. Scientists say rising ocean temperatures from climate change and a strong El Niño pattern are supercharging these storms. The region is also still struggling to recover from Super Typhoon Sinlaku, which caused $1.5 billion in damage in April.

What to watch next

Watch for local government damage assessments on Tuesday, July 7, to see the scale of destruction on Rota, and monitor whether the US federal government declares a major disaster to release recovery funds.

Words to know

Super Typhoon SOO-per tye-FOON
A tropical cyclone in the western Pacific Ocean with sustained winds of 240 kilometers per hour or more, equivalent to a Category 4 or 5 hurricane.
El Niño el NEEN-yoh
A climate pattern characterized by the unusual warming of surface waters in the eastern Pacific Ocean, which affects global weather and makes storms stronger.
Inundation in-un-DAY-shun
Flooding, especially when water covers an area of land that is usually dry.

India orders Meta to disable Instagram ads promoting child sexual abuse material

What happened

India's Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology has ordered Meta to disable Instagram advertisements that promote child sexual abuse material. This action follows an investigation by the BBC showing that Instagram approved paid ads using explicit search terms that redirected users to Telegram channels where they could buy this illegal material. The government has demanded a detailed explanation from Meta within seven days.

Why it matters

Under India's Information Technology Rules, tech platforms must remove such content within 24 hours or risk losing their legal immunity, which protects them from being held responsible for user-generated content. Meta claims it has a zero-tolerance policy and uses artificial intelligence to find this content, but admits it struggles to stop criminals among its billions of users.

What to watch next

Watch for Meta's formal response to the Indian government's notice by Saturday, July 11, and whether India's cybercrime units launch criminal prosecutions against local advertisers.

Words to know

Intermediary in-ter-MEE-dee-air-ee
A company, like a social media platform, that hosts or transmits communication and content created by other people.
Due diligence doo DIL-ih-juhns
The reasonable steps and care a company is legally required to take to avoid harm or follow regulations.

China releases underground church pastor Jin Mingri following US intervention

What happened

Chinese authorities have released Pastor Jin Mingri, the founder of one of China's largest unregistered underground Christian churches, who was detained during a major crackdown last October. Jin has arrived safely in Los Angeles after US President Donald Trump raised his detention directly with Chinese President Xi Jinping during a state visit in May. While Jin's family celebrated his release as a miracle, human rights groups note that at least eight other members of his church remain imprisoned.

Why it matters

China's ruling Communist Party is officially atheist and requires all religious groups to register with the state, which uses state-approved leaders to ensure loyalty to the party. Zion Church, which Jin founded, was banned in 2018 after resisting government orders to install security cameras in its sanctuary. Jin's release highlights how personal, high-level diplomacy between the US and China can sometimes override Beijing's strict domestic security policies.

What to watch next

Watch whether US President Donald Trump makes a statement about the release, and whether the US continues to press for the release of Hong Kong pro-democracy publisher Jimmy Lai, whose case Trump also raised.

Words to know

Underground church UN-der-ground cherch
A Christian congregation in China that operates without official government registration and control.
Sinicize SIN-ih-syze
To make something more Chinese or bring it under the influence of Chinese culture and the ruling Communist Party's ideology.

Tensions rise between Nigeria and South Africa over anti-migrant violence

What happened

Nigeria's foreign ministry has strongly condemned South Africa over the recent deaths of two Nigerian nationals, warning that foreigners are being unduly targeted during intense anti-migrant protests. One Nigerian was allegedly killed by South African metro police using harsh interrogation methods, while another was killed outside his shop by unidentified attackers. These deaths happened just before a June 30 deadline set by South African protest groups demanding that undocumented foreigners leave the country.

Why it matters

Tensions between the two countries are flaring as South African cabinet ministers have refused to pay compensation to departing foreigners and made comments linking Nigerians to drug dealing. South Africa is facing high unemployment and economic strain, leading to over 120 nationwide anti-migrant rallies on June 30 alone. In response, several countries, including Nigeria, Ghana, and Malawi, have already repatriated thousands of their citizens.

What to watch next

Watch for South Africa's official diplomatic response to Nigeria's formal warning, and whether Nigeria takes the matter to the African Union for regional arbitration.

Words to know

Repatriate ree-PAY-tree-ayt
To send or bring someone back to their own country of citizenship.
Xenophobic zee-nuh-FOH-bik
Showing a strong dislike or fear of people from other countries.
Apartheid uh-PAR-tyte
A historical system of institutionalized racial segregation and discrimination in South Africa, which Nigeria compared to South Africa's current treatment of foreigners.

Torrential monsoon rains disrupt transport and prompt red alerts across India

What happened

Monsoon rains have intensified across India, killing at least three people in Maharashtra and disrupting air, road, and rail transport. The India Meteorological Department (IMD) has issued red alerts, warning of extremely heavy rain for Maharashtra, Chhattisgarh, and eastern Gujarat. This sudden surge in rainfall is driven by the season's first depression, a low-pressure weather system that formed over the Bay of Bengal and is moving inland.

Why it matters

While India ended June with a 30% rainfall deficit, the heavy July rains have rapidly reduced this shortage, bringing relief to farmers but causing severe urban flooding. In Mumbai, schools and colleges were closed after some areas received over 260 mm of rain in 24 hours, which also forced a temporary halt to airport runway operations.

What to watch next

Watch for the IMD's daily weather updates on Tuesday, July 7, to see if the depression weakens as it moves across Chhattisgarh, and check whether schools in Mumbai and Thane reopen on Wednesday.

Words to know

Depression dee-PRESH-un
In meteorology, a system of low atmospheric pressure that often brings strong winds and heavy rain.
Red alert red uh-LERT
The highest level of weather warning issued by Indian authorities, advising emergency agencies and the public to take immediate action to protect lives.
Monsoon mon-SOON
A seasonal wind pattern in South Asia that brings heavy summer rains, which are essential for agriculture but often cause flooding.

Turkey detains activists and journalists ahead of hosting first NATO summit in 22 years

What happened

Turkish authorities have arrested dozens of journalists, lawyers, left-wing activists, and suspected militants in a major security sweep across the country. The raids come as Turkey prepares to host its first NATO summit in 22 years, which starts on Tuesday, July 7, in the capital city of Ankara. While the government claims the arrests are part of counterterrorism investigations, critics argue they are meant to silence protests and dissent before world leaders arrive.

Why it matters

Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdogan has banned public gatherings in Ankara, but protesters still demonstrated against NATO on Sunday. The summit itself will test the alliance's unity as NATO's new leader, Mark Rutte, attempts to keep US President Donald Trump committed to European defense amidst reviews of US troop deployments in Europe. Turkey is also trying to use the summit to boost its own standing and influence within the 32-member military alliance.

What to watch next

Watch for the start of the NATO summit on Tuesday, July 7, in Ankara, and monitor whether any major defense contracts or weapons-production deals are announced.

Words to know

NATO NAY-toh
The North Atlantic Treaty Organization, a political and military alliance of 32 North American and European countries established to guarantee the freedom and security of its members.
Counterterrorism kown-ter-TEHR-or-iz-um
Activities and security operations designed to prevent or combat terrorism.
Procurement proh-KYOOR-munt
The process of finding and purchasing goods, services, or weapons, especially by a government or military.
