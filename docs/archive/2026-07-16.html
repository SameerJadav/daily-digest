Landmark India-UK free trade agreement takes effect

What happened

A major free trade agreement between India and the United Kingdom officially came into force on Wednesday. The agreement removes import taxes on nearly 99% of Indian exports entering the British market, spanning sectors like textiles, agriculture, and machinery. Prime Minister Narendra Modi welcomed the deal, stating it would significantly deepen economic and social ties between the two nations.

Why it matters

India and the UK have been negotiating this landmark deal for years to boost bilateral commerce. It makes Indian products highly competitive in the UK and will also phase out import tariffs on British goods like medical devices and automotive parts in India.

What to watch next

Watch for the upcoming investment roadshows in the UK scheduled by Indian states like Karnataka, and monitor when the first duty-free cargo shipments land in British ports.

Words to know

Free trade agreement free-TRADE uh-GREE-muhnt
A pact between two or more nations to reduce barriers to imports and exports among them. In this story, it removes import duties on almost all trade between India and the UK.
Bilateral by-LAT-uh-ruhl
Involving two parties or countries. Here, it refers to the economic and trade relations negotiated between India and the United Kingdom.
MSMEs em-ess-em-EEz
An acronym for Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises, which are small-scale businesses. This agreement is expected to open new export opportunities for these Indian businesses.

UPDATE: Iran shuts Strait of Hormuz amid fresh US strikes, prompting Indian maritime tracking policy

What happened

Iran has shut the vital Strait of Hormuz to all shipping and launched retaliatory missile strikes on US bases in neighbouring Gulf nations, following heavy US airstrikes. In response to these escalations and the confirmed death of a ninth Indian sailor, India's Shipping Ministry has ordered the creation of a real-time tracking dashboard for all Indian seafarers worldwide and dedicated liaison officers for affected families. This escalation follows the US reinstating its naval blockade of Iranian ports, which we reported on yesterday, and means the June ceasefire is now completely void.

Why it matters

The Strait of Hormuz is a global chokepoint through which 20% of the world's oil and gas passes, and its closure has already pushed global oil prices to a one-month high. With thousands of Indian sailors working on international vessels in these volatile waters, the Indian government is stepping in to ensure no sailor is forced to navigate dangerous zones without protection.

What to watch next

Watch for whether the United Nations Security Council or regional powers can broker an emergency dialogue to reopen the strait, and monitor the launch of India's new seafarer tracking dashboard.

Words to know

Chokepoint CHOHK-poynt
A narrow route, such as a canal or strait, where shipping traffic can easily be blocked or congested. In this story, the Strait of Hormuz is a key chokepoint for global energy deliveries.
Liaison officer lee-AY-on OFF-ih-ser
A person who coordinates communication between two organizations or groups. The Indian government is assigning one to each affected seafarer's family to handle financial, legal, and travel assistance.

China's economic growth slows to 4.3% in second quarter

What happened

China's economy grew by 4.3% between April and June, falling short of Beijing's annual target of 4.5% to 5% and marking its slowest expansion since 2022. While China's export sector remains booming—with car exports exceeding one million in June—domestic spending has plunged, with local car sales dropping by 16% and consumer spending remaining weak.

Why it matters

As the world's second-largest economy, China's slowdown has major global implications. Beijing's heavy state subsidies have turned it into an export powerhouse, but a prolonged property market slump and high unemployment have left Chinese consumers afraid to spend money.

What to watch next

Watch for the meeting of top Chinese Communist Party officials later this month to see if they announce new economic stimulus packages, and monitor the IMF's economic growth projections for China heading into 2027.

Words to know

Gross domestic product grohs doh-MES-tik PROD-uhkt
The total monetary value of all finished goods and services produced within a country's borders in a specific time period. It serves as a broad scorecard of a nation's economic health.
Stimulus STIM-yuh-luhs
Government actions, such as tax cuts or spending on public projects, designed to kick-start economic activity. Economists believe China needs these measures to encourage local consumers to spend.
Fixed-asset investment fikst-ASS-et in-VEST-muhnt
Spending on physical assets that are used long-term, like factories, machinery, roads, and bridges. This spending fell sharply in China, signaling a slowdown in local development.

Spain and Gibraltar remove border fence, ending decades of controls

What happened

Workers fully dismantled the border fence separating Spain and the British overseas territory of Gibraltar on Wednesday. The move follows a historic post-Brexit treaty signed in Brussels, which officially admits Gibraltar into the European Union's Schengen free-travel zone.

Why it matters

Spain has claimed sovereignty over Gibraltar since ceding it to Britain in 1713, and the border has been a source of heavy political tension and daily traffic jams for over a century. The agreement allows over 15,000 Spanish commuters and thousands of Gibraltar residents to cross the border daily without passport checks.

What to watch next

Watch for Spanish Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez's planned visit to the border zone later on Wednesday, and monitor how the joint passport checks by both Spanish and Gibraltarian officers at Gibraltar's airport function in the coming weeks.

Words to know

Schengen Area SHENG-uhn AIR-ee-uh
A zone made up of European countries that have officially abolished passport and all other types of border control at their mutual borders. Gibraltar is now joining this zone to allow free movement with Spain.
Cede seed
To formally yield or give up control of a territory to another country. Spain yielded control of Gibraltar to Britain in 1713.

French parliament approves landmark assisted dying bill

What happened

France's National Assembly approved a historic bill on Wednesday that establishes a legal right to assisted dying for terminally ill adults. The law allows patients suffering from 'serious and incurable' physical illnesses to request and administer a lethal substance, with doctors or nurses stepping in only if the patient is physically unable to do so.

Why it matters

The issue has sparked intense moral and political debates in France for years, drawing fierce opposition from the Catholic Church and conservative lawmakers. If the bill clears its final legal hurdles, France will join a small group of nations, including the Netherlands and Canada, that allow the practice.

What to watch next

Watch for the ruling of France's Constitutional Council, which will review the bill's legality, and monitor the UK Parliament's upcoming debate on its own assisted dying bill in September.

Words to know

Assisted dying uh-SIST-ed DY-ing
The practice of allowing terminally ill individuals to end their own lives with medical assistance. Under the new French bill, it is restricted to adults facing unbearable physical pain.
Constitutional Council kon-stih-TOO-shuh-nuhl KOWN-suhl
France's highest constitutional authority, which checks whether laws comply with the country's constitution. It has the power to reject or amend sections of the newly passed bill.
National Assembly NASH-uh-nuhl uh-SEM-blee
The lower house of the French parliament. It passed the bill despite the upper house, the Senate, repeatedly rejecting it.

Ukraine's President Zelenskyy ousts popular defence minister in cabinet reshuffle

What happened

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy has dismissed his highly popular defence minister, Mykhailo Fedorov, as part of a sweeping government reshuffle. The 35-year-old minister was widely praised for curbing military corruption, deploying advanced battlefield drones, and testing Ukraine's first high-accuracy ballistic missile.

Why it matters

Fedorov's sudden removal has sparked anger among Ukrainian civil society, opposition politicians, and Western allies. Analysts suggest Zelenskyy is reshuffling his cabinet to distance his administration from recent military draft scandals and to centralise political control following the resignation of his prime minister.

What to watch next

Watch for the Ukrainian parliament to vote on Serhiy Koretskyi, the head of the state energy company Naftogaz, who is expected to be named the new prime minister, and see if Svyrydenko is confirmed as Ukraine's new ambassador to the United States.

Words to know

Cabinet reshuffle KAB-ih-nit ree-SHUF-uhl
A process where a head of government changes the ministers who lead various departments. Zelenskyy ordered this to reset his administration's image amidst military scandals.
Procurement proh-KYOOR-muhnt
The act of obtaining goods or services, typically for a government or military. The outgoing minister was credited with saving billions of dollars by cleaning up this system in Ukraine.
