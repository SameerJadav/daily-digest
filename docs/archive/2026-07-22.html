UPDATE: Ukrainian President Zelenskyy dismisses military chief Oleksandr Syrskyi amid ongoing protests

What happened

Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelenskyy has fired his military commander-in-chief, Oleksandr Syrskyi, and replaced him with the younger, innovative General Mykhailo Drapatyi. Zelenskyy also offered a new technology-focused government position to Mykhailo Fedorov, the popular former defence minister. This major reshuffle comes after six days of nationwide street protests demanding Syrskyi's removal and Fedorov's return.

Why it matters

This is one of the biggest political crises in Ukraine since Russia's 2022 invasion. Protesters accused the Soviet-trained Syrskyi of blocking military modernization, while praising the 35-year-old Fedorov for introducing advanced drones and data analysis on the battlefield. By bringing in Drapatyi, an ally of Fedorov, Zelenskyy hopes to defuse public anger and reform the military's command structure.

What to watch next

Watch for the Ukrainian parliament's upcoming votes to formally confirm the new military appointments and details of Fedorov's new role. Monitor whether the street protests subside in response to these leadership changes. Track how the new commander, General Drapatyi, implements the promised reorganisation of the army's command and drone strategy.

Words to know

Soviet-style command SOH-vee-et style kuh-MAND
A rigid, top-down military leadership system originating from the former Soviet Union. Critics in Ukraine accused the dismissed general of using this outdated style instead of modernizing the military.
Incursion in-KER-zhun
A sudden, brief invasion of enemy territory. It refers to the surprise Ukrainian military advance into Russia's Kursk region led by General Syrskyi in 2024.

At least 20 dead in gas explosion and tunnel collapse at Sikkim hydroelectric project

What happened

A suspected methane gas explosion has killed at least 20 workers and left five others trapped inside an under-construction tunnel in Namchi district, Sikkim. The blast occurred during a routine inspection inside a seven-kilometer tunnel for the Teesta Stage-VI Hydroelectric Project. National and state disaster response teams are working around the clock to stabilize the smoke-filled environment and rescue the remaining trapped personnel.

Why it matters

The tragedy highlights the extreme physical and environmental dangers of constructing large-scale infrastructure projects in the Himalayan region. It has drawn immediate political attention, with Sikkim's Chief Minister launching a high-level inquiry and Prime Minister Narendra Modi pledging federal assistance and financial relief for the victims' families.

What to watch next

Watch for the newly formed high-level inquiry committee to release its findings on the cause of the gas leak. Monitor rescue updates to see if the five trapped personnel are safely retrieved from the tunnel. Track when the state and central governments distribute the announced compensation funds to the affected families.

Words to know

Methane MEE-theyn
A highly flammable, odorless gas often trapped in deep rock formations. It is suspected to have leaked and exploded inside the under-construction tunnel in Sikkim.
Head race tunnel HED reys TUN-uhl
A specialized channel built to carry water under high pressure from a reservoir to an electricity-generating powerhouse. It is the type of tunnel that collapsed in Sikkim.

France becomes first European Union nation to pass social media ban for children under 15

What happened

France's parliament has passed a landmark law that completely bans children under the age of 15 from using social media platforms. Starting September 1, under-15s will be blocked from creating new accounts, and social media companies must close all existing accounts of children under that age by January 2027. The law also bans mobile phones in high schools and requires platforms to use age-verification systems.

Why it matters

France is the first EU country to pass such a sweeping ban, setting a major precedent as other European nations like Greece and Austria debate similar laws. The move follows warnings from public health watchdogs about the severe impacts of platforms like TikTok and Instagram on youth mental health, including depression and self-harm.

What to watch next

Watch for the French Constitutional Council's upcoming ruling on whether the law violates the constitution before its planned September rollout. Monitor whether the European Commission introduces EU-wide age restrictions for minors later this year. Track how social media companies respond to the requirement of using France's new age-verification app.

Words to know

Blanket ban BLANG-kit ban
A complete, universal prohibition that applies to everyone or everything in a category. It refers to France's total prohibition of social media for children under 15.
Age-verification eyj ver-ih-fih-KEY-shun
A process or software tool used to confirm that a digital user meets a minimum age requirement. French laws will require platforms to use these tools to block children.

At least 41 dead after overloaded passenger ferry capsizes off Guyana

What happened

At least 41 people have drowned and dozens remain missing after an 87-year-old passenger ferry, the MV Barima, capsized off the coast of Guyana. The vessel was carrying 179 people—far more than its official manifest of 133—when it was hit by a large wave and quickly took on water. Police have detained the ferry's captain and a crew member after they tested positive for marijuana.

Why it matters

This is Guyana's worst maritime disaster in decades, exposing severe gaps in safety regulations, overcrowding, and crew conduct. French military divers from neighboring French Guiana have joined local teams in a desperate search through the remote, rainforest-adjacent waters to locate survivors and bodies.

What to watch next

Watch for the findings of the official government investigation into criminal negligence by the ferry operators. Monitor the ongoing search-and-rescue operation to determine the final death toll of the disaster. Track whether the detained captain and crew members are formally charged in court.

Words to know

Passenger manifest PAS-in-jer MAN-ih-fest
An official list of all travelers and crew members onboard a commercial vessel. In this story, the ferry was dangerously overloaded with dozens of unregistered passengers.
Maritime MA-rih-tym
Relating to sea travel, shipping, or naval activity. It describes the ferry disaster as Guyana's worst shipping accident in decades.
Essequibo ess-eh-KEE-boh
A mineral-rich territory in western Guyana that is currently claimed by neighboring Venezuela. The ill-fated ferry was traveling toward this inland region before it turned over.

UPDATE: Yemen's Houthis declare naval blockade of vital Bab el-Mandeb strait

What happened

Yemen's Iran-backed Houthi rebels have declared a naval blockade of the strategic Bab el-Mandeb strait, threatening to attack any cargo or oil tankers that visit Saudi Arabian ports. The Houthis claim the blockade is retaliation for a recent Saudi air strike on an airport in Yemen's capital. In immediate response to the threat, at least two large oil tankers carrying Saudi crude to India and China have already reversed course.

Why it matters

This blockade directly escalates the regional conflict, which began when the Houthis ended a four-year truce with Saudi Arabia last week. With the neighboring Strait of Hormuz already closed due to the US-Iran war, this new blockade threatens to completely choke off Saudi oil exports, which could destabilize global energy markets and drive up fuel prices in India.

What to watch next

Watch for whether Saudi Arabia launches retaliatory military strikes against Houthi targets in Yemen. Monitor whether the US administration escalates military operations or pursues a 10-day ceasefire to allow diplomatic talks. Track if global shipping companies decide to halt all transits through the Red Sea.

Words to know

Bab el-Mandeb bab el-MAN-deb
A narrow, strategic strait connecting the Red Sea to the Gulf of Aden. It is a vital global shipping route now blockaded by Yemen's Houthi rebels.
Militia mih-LISH-uh
An armed rebel group or citizens' army that is not part of a country's official military. It refers to the Iran-backed Houthi forces controlling northern Yemen.

UPDATE: Rahul Gandhi and other opposition MPs detained in Delhi as paper leak protests escalate

What happened

Delhi Police forcibly removed and detained India's opposition leader Rahul Gandhi and other top opposition lawmakers after they staged a surprise sit-in outside Prime Minister Narendra Modi's residence. The leaders, who tied their wrists with plastic zip ties to resist removal, were protesting a violent police crackdown on student demonstrators. The politicians were released late Tuesday night after being held at various locations.

Why it matters

We were previously watching for the government's response to youth-led protests demanding the resignation of Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan over medical exam paper leaks. By taking the fight directly to the prime minister's doorstep, the opposition has unified with the student movement, turning a youth protest into a major national political crisis that threatens to paralyze the ongoing parliament session.

What to watch next

Watch for the opposition parties to attempt a major debate on the paper leaks inside Parliament on Wednesday. Monitor whether student groups continue their protests in the capital despite the police crackdown. Track if the government takes any disciplinary action against police officers accused of brutality.

Words to know

Sit-in SIT-in
A form of peaceful protest where people occupy an area and refuse to leave until their demands are addressed. It refers to the demonstration staged by opposition leaders directly outside the Prime Minister's residence.
Zip tie ZIP ty
A strong, plastic fastener commonly used by police as temporary handcuffs. Rahul Gandhi distributed these to fellow protesters to make it harder for the police to carry them away.
